THE AUSTRALIAN CRAFT BEER REVOLUTION

BY JING YI TEOH

An interactive analysis of Australia's shifting palate: what the national drinking survey says we prefer, and what the GABS Hottest 100 2025 reveals about the beers Australians vote for.

The National Palate

Before exploring the niche world of craft beer, we must understand the broader Australian context.

The 'Beer Gap' is most evident in the 18–24 cohort, where preference sits at just 16.1%—nearly half that of the 45–54 demographic. This isn't just a lack of interest; it's a pivot toward variety.

This 'Generation Gap' represents the primary growth engine for the Craft Revolution: a demographic that rejects commercial staples in favor of the diverse, innovative flavor profiles found in independent breweries.

Mapping the Heartland

The craft beer revolution isn't evenly distributed. Geographic analysis shows distinct hotspots of brewing excellence across the country. The density of breweries tells a story of urban hubs and regional craft pioneers.

Eastern Dominance. Victoria and New South Wales are the massive powerhouses in the Australian craft industry. Notice the dense clusters around Melbourne and Sydney.

Quality Over Quantity. Some states have fewer breweries but produce a massive number of top 100 beers. It's not just about how many breweries exist, but their impact.

What Makes a "Hottest 100" Beer?

Diving into the acclaimed GABS Hottest 100, we uncover the styles, rank momentum, and brewers that define the pinnacle of Australian craft brewing.

Pale Ales and IPAs completely dominate the landscape, showcasing Australia's distinct thirst for hoppy, aromatic profiles. The sheer volume of these styles demonstrates a clear market preference.

For the rank-density charts below, each dot is one beer. Smaller rank numbers are better, so dots further left are higher-performing beers. The black ticks mark the median rank for each style, giving a quick centre point without needing statistical jargon.

The Final Verdict

Synthesising the analysis of the Hottest 100 reveals the overall impact and a state-by-state breakdown.

Australia's palate is evolving. While national trends remain rooted in traditional wine and beer, the GABS 100 reveals a clear shift toward regional, independent diversity, driven by New South Wales and Victoria. We are moving away from mass production toward a culture that values local innovation and "hazy" craft styles.
